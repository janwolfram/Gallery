Ronald Ramp builds tower for his stamp collection

Ronald Ramp, the owner of the largest stamp collection in the world, recently inaugurated a building to host his hobby. Called "Stamp Tower", the 100-storey building is right in the center of Little Pear. It's impressive interior is mostly made up of gold and marble - with some diamonds in between. Along the walls are fountains of all sizes and shapes. A huge chandelier hangs from the ceiling. Servants are bustling about the hall, carrying exotic fruits and drinks.

The first thing visitors see after passing through the great mahogany doors, though, is a platinum throne adorned with rubies and other gems. And sitting on it is the king of the castle himself, Ronald Ramp. Many people tried to copy his style, but nobody ever achieved the look of confidence and wisdom his haircut grants him.

I approached him with awe, insanely grateful for the invitation I got just the day before. It said, he'd seen my ad on Twitter and he wanted to have some photos taken for his profile. I was nervous. What am I supposed to say to such a man? Trying to get things going, I decided to go with a plain and simple "Howdy, whaddup?!".
